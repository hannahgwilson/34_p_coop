### Slide 6 — Capital Plan 2026–2030
- **Purpose:** Show the 5-year road ahead and explicitly own the board's biggest discretionary choice.

- **Headline:** 5 years, ~$2.19M of capital work. Two big items dominate (elevator and lobby); everything else is compliance and maintenance.

- **🟧 Board decision to call out: Sidewalks — Targeted Tile Repair (not full replacement)**
  - Full sidewalk replacement: **~$300K** in 2026
  - Targeted tile repair (selected): **~$35K** in 2026
  - **Savings: ~$265K**, redirected to preserve reserve capacity ahead of the elevator project
  - **Why targeted:** the sidewalks are structurally sound; only specific tiles show wear. Full replacement would have been premature, crowded out the 2027 elevator funding, and offered no compliance benefit (LL126 garage work is separate and already complete).
  - **Reversibility:** if conditions deteriorate, the full replacement remains available as a future capital item without penalty.
  - *Treat this as a deliberate, defensible board call — not a deferral.*

- **Project list, 5-year horizon:**
  - **2026 — $279K:** FISP/LL11 SAFE filing $25K · Sidewalks (targeted tile repair) $35K · Professional space buildout $25K · Garage balance $44K · General capital maintenance $150K
  - **2027 — $850K:** Elevator modernization, year 1 $700K (funded by the deferred 2025 assessment) · General capital maintenance $150K
  - **2028 — $450K:** Elevator modernization, year 2 $300K · General capital maintenance $150K
  - **2029 — $350K:** Lobby HVAC $50K · Lobby renovation $150K · General capital maintenance $150K
  - **2030 — $263K:** G-Level $100K · LL11 proposal $13K · General capital maintenance $150K

- **Other notes worth surfacing:**
  - Garage SREM/LL126 ($196K) was **paid in 2025** — already off the 2026 plan.
  - **Major FISP/Local Law 11 façade work is deferred to the next cycle (est. 2031–2032)** — the 2026 spend is the SAFE filing only.
  - The plan introduces a **$150K/yr General Capital Maintenance allowance** (based on 2023 actuals ~$131K + inflation buffer) — new structural line we should explain in the talk track.

- **Compliance backdrop:** LL11 (façade), LL87 (energy audit complete), LL88 (lighting), LL97 (Climate Mobilization Act — ~$13K/yr starting 2024, steeper 2030+), LL126 (garage, completed 2025).

- **Source:** Capital Plan 2026+ — Targeted Tile Repair scenario (March 2026); Audited Note 16.

- **Visual:** Stacked column by year, color-coded by project category. Add a **dedicated orange callout box** for the sidewalk decision: "Full replacement $300K → Targeted repair $35K · $265K redirected to elevator funding."

### Slide 7 — Reserves Hold Through the Plan; 2028 Refi Is the Variable to Watch
- **Purpose:** Answer "do we have enough?" with the **targeted-repair** scenario numbers and name the real risk.
- **Scenario in use:** Capital Plan 2026+ — **Targeted Tile Repair ($30–35K sidewalks)**. Assumptions: opening reserve $1,141,745 (audited 12/31/25), $262.5K capital contribution growing 5%/yr, $150K/yr general capital maintenance allowance, mortgage refi 12/1/2028.
- **5-year reserve trajectory:**

  | YE | Project spending | Ending reserve | 3-mo maintenance bench | Coverage |
  |---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
  | 2026 | $279K | **$1.31M** | $563K | **233%** |
  | 2027 | $850K | **$769K** | $591K | **130%** |
  | 2028 | $450K | **$610K** | $621K | **98%** |
  | 2029 | $350K | $564K | $652K | 87% |
  | 2030 | $263K | $620K | $684K | 91% |

- **What this means:** the existing **$262.5K/yr capital contribution** (escalating ~5%/yr) is correctly sized to fund the targeted-repair plan. **Reserves stay at or above the 3-month operating benchmark through 2028.** The 2029 dip is roughly **$88K** at the trough, with the trajectory already recovering by 2030 — **no new assessment is required to execute this plan.**
- **The real variable: the 2028 mortgage maturity.** $5.618M comes due Dec 1, 2028 (Apple Bank, 3.225% interest-only). Refinancing at today's rates means a structurally higher debt-service line — every 1% of rate adds ~$56K/yr, roughly **1 point of maintenance**. Refi planning kicks off 2027.
- **Tools available to the board if conditions change:**
  - Continue the $262.5K/yr capital contribution (programmed, escalating)
  - $350K unused revolving line of credit at Apple Bank — bridge, not solution
  - 2028 refinancing window — could include modest cash-out to top up reserves
  - A future capital assessment, only if the refi outcome or capital cadence requires one
- **Source:** Capital Plan 2026+ — Targeted Tile Repair scenario (March 2026), Reserve Cash Flow block; Audited Note 7 (mortgage); Budget PDF p.5 (maintenance history).
- **Visual:** Combo chart — bars for ending reserve, line for 3-month benchmark, 2026–2030; 2028 mortgage marker; small "Targeted Tile Repair scenario" tag in the corner.

### Slide 8 — Summary & Q&A
- **Purpose:** Three takeaways and the door open for questions.
- **Key points:**
  - Operations are healthy; 2026 is budgeted to breakeven with a 5.5% maintenance increase.
  - Reserves more than doubled in 2025 and we now have a permanent $262.5K/yr capital contribution funding the future.
  - Under the updated capital plan, reserves stay at or above the 3-month operating benchmark through 2028. The variable to watch is the **2028 mortgage refinancing**, not the capital plan itself — the board will start communicating refi planning in 2027.
- **Visual:** Three short bullets + treasurer contact for follow-up.
